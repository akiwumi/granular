# Granular — Pitch Deck Update
> 20 slides · Investor Presentation · April 2026

---

## Slide 1 — Title

**Granular**

See not just where your money went, but exactly what you bought.

PRIVACY-FIRST PERSONAL FINANCE | DIGITAL RECEIPT INFRASTRUCTURE | ITEM-LEVEL SPENDING INTELLIGENCE

---

## Slide 2 — The One-Line Summary

**Granular is a privacy-first spending app that turns receipts and purchases into item-level financial intelligence.**

Most finance apps tell users the merchant and the total.

Granular tells users:

- what they bought
- where they bought it
- which card they used where available
- how the basket affected their spending
- which products, stores, and habits are changing over time

The product starts with receipt scanning and digital receipt import, then becomes more automated through payment matching and retailer integrations.

---

## Slide 3 — The Problem

**Personal finance apps still stop too early.**

A banking app may show:

- Tesco — £84.20
- Amazon — £31.50
- Boots — £18.40

But that does not answer the questions users actually have:

- What did I buy?
- Which products pushed me over budget?
- Which retailer is becoming more expensive for me?
- What did I spend across all my cards?
- Why does my money feel tighter this week?

Merchant-level transaction data is useful, but it is not enough for practical financial control.

---

## Slide 4 — The User Pain

**Users are trying to manage real spending with incomplete information.**

Everyday spending is messy:

- receipts are paper, emailed, lost, or spread across retailers
- purchases happen across multiple cards and accounts
- bank feeds can lag behind real activity
- categories are too broad to explain actual behavior
- households need answers about products, not just merchants

The result is a familiar problem: users know money moved, but they do not know what changed in their actual life and budget.

---

## Slide 5 — The Insight

**The real financial event is not the payment. It is the basket.**

A payment confirms that money moved.

A basket explains why.

Granular makes the basket the core financial record:

- retailer
- date and time
- items
- quantities
- prices
- discounts
- payment method where available
- spending impact

This turns a flat transaction into a useful personal finance object.

---

## Slide 6 — What Granular Is

**Granular is a receipt-first personal finance app.**

It helps users build a clear, searchable, item-level record of everyday spending while keeping detailed purchase data under their control.

The app combines:

- receipt scan and upload
- digital receipt import
- optional card connection
- optional bank connection or manual balance
- item-level purchase history
- budgets, alerts, and spending controls
- exports for records, planning, and reporting
- future retailer API integrations

The first version is useful without waiting for a full retailer network.

## Slide 7 — What The App Does

**Granular turns everyday purchases into organized financial understanding.**

For each purchase, the app can show:

- the store
- the purchase date
- the full item list
- item prices and quantities
- the total
- the card used where available

Across purchases, the app helps users see:

## Slide 8 — Detailed User Flow: Onboarding

**The app starts by giving the user control before asking for deeper access.**

1. User creates an account or starts in local-first mode
2. App explains that detailed purchase records are stored locally by default
3. User chooses how to begin:
   - scan a paper receipt
   - import a digital receipt
   - enter a manual starting balance
   - connect cards where supported
4. User sees value from the first receipt before deeper setup is required
5. Optional bank or card linking is introduced as an enhancement, not a gate

This keeps onboarding simple and reduces trust friction.

---

## Slide 9 — Detailed User Flow: Receipt Capture

**The first core action is adding a receipt.**

A user can:

- scan a paper receipt with the phone camera
- upload a saved image or PDF
- import an email or digital receipt
- receive a structured receipt automatically from supported retailers later

Granular then:

- reads the retailer name, date, total, and line items
- identifies products, quantities, prices, and discounts
- asks the user to confirm unclear items
- saves the receipt as a searchable purchase record
- updates the spending view and related budgets

The user goes from loose receipt to useful financial record in one flow.

## Slide 16 — Privacy And Trust

**Trust is a product feature.**

Purchase history reveals intimate information:

- household structure
- financial pressure
- routines
- health-related behavior
- habits and preferences
- location patterns

Granular is designed around a local-first trust promise:

- detailed purchase history stays on the user's phone by default
- the company does not depend on selling personal profiles
- backend systems handle only minimal coordination where needed
- optional sync and integrations should be explicit and consent-led
- users can export or delete their records

The product gives people utility without making surveillance the price of admission.

## Slide 18 — Product Architecture

**Six layers work together.**

| Layer | What It Does |
|---|---|
| Receipt Layer | Scanned, uploaded, and imported receipt records |
| Purchase Layer | Clean itemized baskets, categories, totals, and history |
| Payments Layer | Optional connected cards and transaction references |
| Spending Layer | Manual or connected purchase-aware spending view |
| Retailer API Layer | Automated basket data from supported retailers |
| Privacy Layer | Local-first storage with minimal coordination data |

This architecture lets Granular launch manually, improve with matching, and scale through integrations.
